Tests for quality and performance


Both GRIA and TeitzBart are tested on the following topics:

  • Performance
  • Solution quality

The motivation for the first part is self-evident. The quality of the solution demands some explanation. It is tested due to the fact that neither algorithm is guaranteed to provide an optimal solution. However, both are said to provide good results. The quality is tested by calculating the difference in cost to an optimal solution.

(See this article for description of the problem and algorithms)

Test info

All algorithms were tested on the same data that consisted of a varying number of possible facility locations and demand locations.

The coordinates of locations are random double numbers ranging from 1 to 200. These were created with Javas Random-class.

1. Smaller tests: Brute force vs.GRIA vs.TeitzBart


  • Possible facility locations: 25
  • Demand locations: 500 .
